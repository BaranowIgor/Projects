LOGISTICS APP: PALLET BOX COUNT CALCULATOR

I have created innovative tool that goes beyond traditional box stacking calculators, offering a sophisticated solution for optimizing pallet space. Through the utilization of advanced algorithms, my application excels in optimizing box placement, far outperforming similar calculators from competitors by accommodating a significantly greater number of boxes on each pallet. My app dynamically rotates and positions boxes to find the most efficient layout, even exploring multi-directional stacking when needed. This means you can pack more boxes onto your pallets than ever before, ultimately saving time, space, and resources. Plus, error-proof design ensures that incorrect inputs, such as oversized boxes or excessive weight, are instantly flagged, guaranteeing the integrity of your palletization process.

REDUCED TRANSPORTATION BY 833 KM PER DAY

I searched the database of stores and which DCs are currently serviced and found stores that are not serviced by the nearest DC. But it was not possible to simply assign an additional, closer store to DC. So I was looking for dependencies between DC pairs that could potentially swap stores and thus cover fewer kilometers. After such a search, I found 8 DC pairs with 56 store exchanges that saved 833 km of transportation per day.

DESIGN OF WAREHOUSE PROCESSES AND EQUIPMENT FOR BTS CLASS WAREHOUSES

Based on data on the plot area, maximum height of the building, products to be stored, load weight (tons/year), number of working days in the warehouse, number of work shifts per day, duration of one shift, load inspection time and others, I designed a build-to-suit warehouse and the processes taking place in this center

  • I calculated the daily supply stream, min. stock, cyclical stock, max. stock
  • I chose the most suitable storage methods (ABC, LIFO, FIFO) and racks for each method. Calculated the parameters of the racks to store the planned amount of goods
  • Then types and number of forklifts were selected, where all parameters were also taken into account (max distance of the forlift, time of driving empty forklift forward and backward, time of pick up of the load, load placement time, maximum lifting height, lifting time for forks with load/without load, lowering time with/without load, time of one turn)
  • All the distances and the number of turns that each forklift must make were calculated.
